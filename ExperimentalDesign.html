Effect of Video Games on Heart Rate

by mystith

3 / 8 / 19

Abstract

The purpose of the experiment was to look at the effects of various video games on the body, and determine whether or not the type of game impacts anything. In the experiment, various live streams with attached heart monitors were analyzed for data using an Optical Character Recognition (OCR) application. Horror games were found to have the most effect, with competitive rhythm game osu! following soon after. The experiment was successful in discovering the effect of video games on heart rate, and found that, depending on the game and it’s intensity, heart rate increased.

Background

        Previously, it was noticed that, while playing osu!, a Twitch streamer reached a heart rate of 195 beats per minute while achieving a highly acclaimed play (Twitch, 2019). There have been other occurrences of this as well, with the players reaching 180 beats per minute or above. Most if not all of these were caused by high intensity gameplay.

One study was done where thirty teenage boys were exposed to violent and non violent games, which discovered that there was an increase in heart rate (Ivarsson et al. 2013), with another study from years before that finding that there was a significant increase in both heart rate and blood pressure within a group of 23 men, exposed to video games.

Heart rate is known as the rate at which the heart pumps blood, measured in beats per minute. Blood pressure is measured as two separate numbers, one as the pressure on the walls of vessels while the heart is pumping, and one as pressure on the walls of vessels while the heart is at rest. Blood pressure is measured in millimeters of mercury. These two are affected quite clearly, shown by one study which found that those exposed to low amounts of violent games had a higher heart rate, on average, compared to those exposed to high amounts of violent video games. This showed that a shock of intense gameplay causes increase in heart rate, in a sense, while continued exposure will cause lower heart rate on average. This is due to how, when nervous, angry, or excited, a chemical known as adrenaline is released, which speeds up respiratory and heart rates. The goal of this experiment is to help find variations in heart rate based on game, and whether or not level of horror or other variables within the game would affect bodily conditions.

Hypothesis

        The heart rate of subjects playing more intense games will increase depending on the intensity of the game they are playing.

Materials

  • Tesseract OCR Engine
  • Various Twitch livestreams
  • Computer
  • Heart Rate Monitor (namely, Fitbit)

Instructions

  1. Turn on and wear Fitbit watch, then set it for a run and begin the gameplay session.
  2. After playing the video games, turn off the Fitbit and go to the Fitbit dashboard.
  3. Download the parse the TCX file from the run.
  4. Add TCX data to spreadsheet.
  5. Download livestreams
  6. Use Tesseract OCR Engine along with grayscaling and noise reduction to read heart rate data from the frame files.
  7. Read the 80th percentile and 20th percentiles from the spreadsheet, then subtract the 80th from the 20th in order to get deviations for each game. Graph these.

Results

        In the results, it can be seen that less intense games such as Street Fighter V, a fighting game, or osu!, a competitive rhythm game, tend to have lower means. while higher intensity games such as Amnesia: A Machine for Pigs, a purely horror game, have significantly higher means. Five out of six of the highest on the chart are all horror games, while the two lowest are not.

        

* NH = Not Horror, H = Horror, NHI = Not Horror; Intense, C = Competitive

Discussion

        The results show a significant increase in heart rate of various video games depending on intensity. With most of the highest heart rates being horror games, it is clear that there is some effect of various types of video games on heart rate. Various studies support this, such as a study where heart rate was monitored between two teenage groups, with various age groups of video games (Jagadheeswari et al. 2018). This study found that there was slight increase in both heart rate and blood pressure. It would’ve been useful to have blood pressure data, but due to the nature of the study, getting blood pressure every 30 seconds would’ve been near impossible. Rust has an unexpectedly high deviation, which could be due to various possible errors. There is a pretty decent amount of error that is possible, but it would be incredibly time consuming to check the data for error due to the sheer amount (in the hundred thousands of points range) of data. This error is caused by inconsistencies in the OCR program, and can only be fixed either by increasing the resolution of the live streams or adding better image processing. It may be possible to specially design AI to read the heart rate, though time consuming. The data collected using the Fitbit is relatively accurate, though there may be inconsistencies due to how the Fitbit measures heart rate, which is known to have some measure of error. This could be improved by making use of a chest heart rate strap, which is known to be more accurate than a wrist strap. There are other factors that could affect results, such as physical health or age, and taking control of those would be preferable.

Conclusion

        Dependent on the game intensity, heart rate will increase, with intense horror games causing higher heart rates while less intense games cause lower heart rates.
